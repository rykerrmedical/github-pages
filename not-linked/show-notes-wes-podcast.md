# Show Notes for Wes Podcast

06:05 [Paper: Ketamine or Etomidate for Tracheal Intubation of Critically Ill Adults](https://references.rykerrmedical.com/Casey2025_Ketamine_Etomidate_RSI)

08:15 HOp Killers - stands for hypoxia (H), oxyegnation (O) & metabolic acidosis (p for pH); the best way to dig in here is by starting at the EmCrit Intubation Checklist(s) and working through the concepts:

EmCrit Ep 92: Intubation Checklist (v1) [view page](https://references.rykerrmedical.com/Weingart2013_RSI_Checklist_v1)

EmCrit Ep 172: Updated EmCrit RSI Checklist (v2) [view page](https://references.rykerrmedical.com/Weingart2013_RSI_Checklist_v2) 

[watch the associated video on youtube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IldjdiR0vKc)

12:00 Dose Adjustment? - The paper cited above ([Casey & friends, 2025](https://references.rykerrmedical.com/Casey2025_Ketamine_Etomidate_RSI)) does look at full, intermediate and low doses of both Ketamine and Etomidate.  And the *Best Practice Guideline on Geriatric Trauma Management* (by [Mangram & friends, 2023](https://references.rykerrmedical.com/ACS2023_Geriatric_Trauma)) discusses dose attenuation by age.

14:15 Wes cites it didn’t pan out in EM and [this ACEP policy paper](https://references.rykerrmedical.com/ACEP2025_ER_Intubation) by Godwin & friends (2023) basically summarizes that take (as well as many, many other takes along the way).

23:00 Clinical Fraility - discussed at length in the Geriatric Guidelines linked above ([Mangram & friends, 2023](https://references.rykerrmedical.com/ACS2023_Geriatric_Trauma)).  Also see here this paper from [Hoogendjik & friends (2019)](https://references.rykerrmedical.com/Hoogendijk2019_Frailty) and this review of a scoring/ assessment tool for the thing ([Shiffer & friends, 2026](https://references.rykerrmedical.com/Shiffer2026_Frailty_Asmt))

27:40 HOP Killers - as mentioned before/ above, see the EmCrit Intubation Checklist(s) to get into the details and context of this idea

32:45 Complications of NCD - best summarized by [this response](https://references.rykerrmedical.com/OpenEvidence_on_NCD) from our AI pal Open Evidence (OE)

39:05 For more on the idea of managing the chest tube to re-inflate the lung, refer to our blog post: Chest Tube Management in Transport

40:40 Paper on Finger Thor vs Chest Tube Placement done by the IDF medics in Gaza: [Mozer-Glassberg & friends, 2025](https://references.rykerrmedical.com/Mozer-Glassberg2025_Gaza_FInger_Thor_vs_CT)

And then [this is what OE has to say](https://references.rykerrmedical.com/OpenEvidence_on_Finger_Thor_vs_CT) about it all.

58:50 BLUE Protocol - the protocol is spelled out in a more in-depth textbook (available in its entirety and [for free via SciHub](https://sci-hub.ru/10.1007/978-3-319-15371-1), also [backed up on archive.org](https://archive.org/details/full_textbooks/Lichtenstein2016_BLUE_Protocol.pdf)), but is reviewed in this brief article by the same author: [Lichtenstein, 2014](https://references.rykerrmedical.com/Lichtenstein2014_BLUE_Summary)

1:00:50 POCUS > CXR for PNA - Just one of a handful of studies on the idea: [Orso & friends, 2025](https://references.rykerrmedical.com/Orso2025_POCUS_vs_CXR_PNA)

1:10:15 To review our discussion with Drew Congdon from SFCC where we discuss his implementation of POCUS in initial paramedic education (among other things), [listen to the podcast here](https://www.rykerrmedical.com/podcast/#drew-and-ryan-discuss-ems-education-at-sfcc-and-in-nm).
